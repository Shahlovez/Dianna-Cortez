More about My Work

It is my mission to develop Performance Art and Theatrical works that are raw, dynamic, imaginative, rich in poetry and complex language, and full of wonder and curiosity. My aim is to create a space to raise consciousness and invite cultural conversations within the NYC community.
Currently I am working on my theatre company's first production. The Black Forest Rose Theatre’s premiere production is Crave by Sarah Kane as it brings consciousness to the Mental Health crisis in the Covid 19 pandemic. It is expecting to be launched in the fall of 2022.

Previously I worked as an actor for the 20% Theatre (a company focused on switching the male/female gender ratios in theatre), Oxford Shakespeare Festival, and City Attic Theatre. I was the Artistic Director and co-creator of City Attic Theatre (C.A.T) whose productions included: Therese Raquin by Neal Bell, On The Verge or The Geography of Yearning by Eric Overmyer, and Marisol by Jose Rivera to name a few. City Attic Theatre also created the New Works Competition, C.A.T. Tales. C.A.T produced the World Premiere of new works The Most Beautiful Lullaby You’ve Ever Heard by Greg Romero and The Cellar by Melissa Gawlowski. The Cellar was selected to perform at Abrons Arts Center’s Festival Samhain and The Strawberry One-Acts Festival in NYC.
As a professional voice and dialect coach, I worked on several productions with The New Group Theatre (Off Broadway) including working with Daniel Sovich, Sadie Scott, and Clhoe Sevigny in Downtown Race Riot; Ed Harris in Buried Child; Ethan Hawke, Vincent D’Onofrio, Zoey Kazan, and Jonathan Marc Sherman in Jonathan’s new play Clive, which was directed by Ethan Hawke; and Ethan Hawke in Hurly Burly. She also worked with Sam Rockwell in the Broadway premiere of A Behanding in Spokane, and Kim Cattrall while she performed the title role in Antony and Cleopatra directed by Janet Suzman at the Liverpool Playhouse in Liverpool, England. She’s coached professional productions On and Off Broadway through the Schoenfeld Theatre, The Beckett Theatre, Luna Stage (in NJ) and Shakespeare On The Sound (in CT)—most recently coaching MacBeth and Hamlet.
I also coached Solo Performance Art pieces: Susan Stein in her one-woman show on the life of Etty Hillesum based on her journals during the Holocaust; Xandra Clark’s Non-binary Production of “Polylogues” which was performed at Dixon Place and The Tank in NYC. Alana Ruban Free’s one-woman show The Beginner at Life, and Yokko Sienkiewicz who won “Best One-Woman Show” in the United Solo Theatre Festival 2014 at Theatre Row for her written/choreographed/devised piece Butoh Medea, and additional devised theatre pieces Hide Your Fires (combining the story of Lady MacBeth with Butoh movement), and In Our Hands with The New Group at L.I.U. Brooklyn students.
