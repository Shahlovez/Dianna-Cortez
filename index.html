About

I am a New York City based actor, singer, director, and a voice/dialect coach. I have coached actors On and Off Broadway. Currently I serve as the Chair of Voice and Speech at Circle in the Square Theatre School, as well as teaching at the Syracuse University/Tepper Program in NYC (B.F.A), PACE University (B.A.-Film/TV/Commercial/Voice Over), The Linklater Center for Voice and Language.

Designated Linklater “Freeing the Natural Voice” Teacher

2012

Linklater voice work is an integrated approach to voice and bodywork. Although most people are born with the ability to express themselves freely, tension and stress over the course of a lifetime, debilitates this ability and limits the potential strength of one's vocal aliveness, and clear articulation. My goal is to revitalize and restore that birthright.

Certified Colaianni Speech/Phonetic Pillows Practitioner

2014

Louis Colaianni Speech work grows seamlessly out of Linklater voice work: Focusing on--Empowerment and Identity through an experiential study of Phonetics.

Services

Open to cooperate as a Performance Artist / Director

As a Performance Artist: I was trained in classical singing and acting, and continued to develop myself with on-going voice, speech, movement lessons and workshops throughout my life. I love working on heightened poetic language that can transform and transport me into rich and unexpected places within me. I believe in embodying the language, so that I can feel the journey in which the writer is intending, and to let it live and become alive inside of me. I, also, believe in being in response to the moment-to-moment interactions with the relationships to others in the play. I enjoy collaborating deeply with the fellow artists in the events that are being developed and shared. I love to develop performance art that integrates imagery, text, music, voice and movement, multi-media, poetry, and song. I, also, have a fondness for classical, modern, and well-written contemporary plays and film.

Voice, Articulation, and Dialect Coach

As a coach, my goal is to work with each client to unlock the uniqueness of their individual and identifiable dialect, and strengthen their access to powerful communication. The Linklater voice work provides keys to unlock hidden tensions that may be inhibiting ones voice from being fully expressed and fully articulated. The exercises are integrated with acting techniques that have been used to ground the actor in their body, voice, and imagination and deepen the experience of language. The speech work continues seamlessly from the foundational voice work, and builds into a transformational process driven development of World Dialects. These techniques can be useful for actors and singers, and applied to any person with a desire to build confidence in their speaking voice.
